Prosperous Farmer

The Prescott Register (Prescott, Kansas) — June 9, 1899

Walter Sutherland, of Sheridan township, was a pleasant caller Wednesday and renewed his supply of reading matter. He came to Kansas in ’66 and located on his present homestead in Sheridan. At that time he had a yoke of oxen and very little money.

By hard work, economy and good management he has amassed a snug fortune. He now owns about 600 acres of rich land divided into five farms, all clear of debts and well improved.

He has made most of his money by raising grain for markets but if the market price did not suit him at harvest time he always held his grain till he could sell at a fair price and in this way became independent and made good profits from his labors.

His family consists of four sons and two daughters, his wife having been dead ten years. One son Will is in Oklahoma farming and running a threshing machine and his other son James is assisting him. Mason is on one of the farms in Stanton township. The youngest son Ray and eldest daughter are at home while the married daughter and husband, Isaac McNabb, are living on one of the farms near the home place.

Mr. Sutherland says he rarely hires help but he and his sons attend to as much of the land as possible and rents the remainder out. This year they have 60 acres of corn and 40 acres of oats on the home place.

Mr. Sutherland is a firm believer in the idea that any man can make a success of his business, be it farming or whatever it is, if he will apply himself and be careful and economical.
